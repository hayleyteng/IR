Document classification</h2>
<p>In this part, the goal is to do classification of the documents based on      <code>UNBIS</code>.<br>
We build algorithm based on      <code>Bidirectional-LSTM</code>, relying on preamble, operatives and title.<br></p>
<h3><a id="user-content-model-and-methodology" class="anchor" aria-hidden="true" href="#model-and-methodology"><svg class="octicon octicon-link" viewBox="0 0 16 16" version="1.1" width="16" height="16" aria-hidden="true"><path fill-rule="evenodd" d="M4 9h1v1H4c-1.5 0-3-1.69-3-3.5S2.55 3 4 3h4c1.45 0 3 1.69 3 3.5 0 1.41-.91 2.72-2 3.25V8.59c.58-.45 1-1.27 1-2.09C10 5.22 8.98 4 8 4H4c-.98 0-2 1.22-2 2.5S3 9 4 9zm9-3h-1v1h1c1 0 2 1.22 2 2.5S13.98 12 13 12H9c-.98 0-2-1.22-2-2.5 0-.83.42-1.64 1-2.09V6.25c-1.09.53-2 1.84-2 3.25C6 11.31 7.55 13 9 13h4c1.45 0 3-1.69 3-3.5S14.5 6 13 6z"></path></svg></a>Model and methodology:</h3>
<blockquote>
<p>Instead of using pre-trained      <code>embedding</code> layer directly, we set up this layer from scratch.<br>
<code>3 LSTMs</code> are applied parallelly. They are expected to deal with preamble, operatives, title separately.<br>
<code>Dropout</code> layer added to fight against overfitting.<br></p>
</blockquote>
